That's actually a really good idea **for the Settler route specifically**.

Right now jobs are:

```text
Pick once
↓
Get +1 stat
↓
Never matter again
```

Which is basically a fancy personality quiz.

If jobs can evolve, they become part of the player's identity.

---

I'd do **2 upgrades**, not 3.

Reason:

```text
Settler Route
15 scenarios total

6 = Job Choice
15 = Route End
```

You only have about 9 scenarios left.

Three promotions would feel absurdly fast.

Greyhaven would be handing out promotions like a startup desperate to retain employees.

## Why This Works

The promotion itself becomes a reward.

Instead of:

```text
Scenario Complete
+1 Trust
```

you get:

```text
Months pass.

Your work has not gone unnoticed.

The treasury offers you a promotion.

Trust +2
```

That feels much more meaningful.

---

```text
6  Job Selection

10 or 11

14

Second Promotion
```

Nice progression.

Not rushed.

Not stagnant.

## Extra Benefit

Now you can write special scenes:

```python
if player["job"] == "archivist":
```

or

```python
if player["job"] == "council_assistant":
```

without creating entirely separate routes.

That's the sweet spot.

Different flavor.

Same overall story.

Very little extra coding.

For v0.4 I'd add:

```python
player["job_rank"] = 1
```

alongside:

```python
player["job"]
```

Future-you can then upgrade:

```text
1 → 2 → 3
```

without redesigning the system later. That's the kind of tiny addition that saves headaches when Scenario 12 suddenly needs to know whether the player is filing books or managing half the archive.
